About me

I am currently an Independent Research Fellow (funded by NSF-Simons) at the Faculty of Arts and Sciences,Harvard. My broad interests are in developing and employing ideas from theory and computation to study biological phenomena. I am very enthusiastic about developing collaborations and leveraging inter-disciplinary approaches to tackle problems.

Apart from my technical work, I am passionate about open science and science communication. In the past, I have collaborated with eLife Community Ambassadors and communication fellows at the MIT ChemE Comm Lab to develop resources, such as the CommKit .

Research Interests
An underlying theme of my work lies in understanding how collective events with many players - be it phase separation, proofreading, or evolution, drives biological specificity. I typically borrow tools from statistical physics, polymer physics, and stochastic processes to model these systems. A brief summary of my current interests can be found below.
Phase separation in transcription

Exploring how transcription machinery condenses into liquid-like droplets at specific regulatory loci, and impact on gene expression and 3D genome structure
